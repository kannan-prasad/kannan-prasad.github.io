Effective Customer Warning (ECW) - HSBC

With fraud and scams increasingly targeting digital payments, HSBC has taken a major step forward in customer protection by launching the Effective Customer Warning (ECW) feature in all market. This feature, now live on HSBC’s mobile app for Me-to-Not-Me (M2NM) domestic payment journeys, provides customers with real-time warnings before completing transactions. By integrating cutting-edge fraud detection technology, ECW strengthens HSBC’s efforts to mitigate payment fraud risks, ensuring that customers are well-informed and better protected against scams.

Seamless Integration of Advanced Fraud Prevention Technology

implement this crucial fraud prevention measure, HSBC introduced a single-page ECW screen into the M2NM domestic payment journey on its mobile app for both iOS and Android. The warning content is dynamically managed using Adobe Experience Manager (AEM), allowing real-time updates without requiring app releases.

The decision to trigger warnings is powered by Feedzai, an advanced fraud detection engine that analyses transactions in real-time based on predefined business analytic model. Additionally, HSBC has leveraged Looker dashboards to monitor the effectiveness of ECW, tracking key metrics such as the number of warnings displayed and whether customers aborted transactions after viewing the warning.

Measurable Impact for Customers and the Bank

This milestone will allow HSBC to measure tangible success metrics, such as the number of fraud attempts prevented and customer response to scam warnings.

This implementation aligns with regulatory expectations, ensuring HSBC continues to meet and exceed compliance standards. Additionally, ECW plays a key role in reducing fraud-related losses by preventing unauthorized transactions, thereby minimizing the need for compensation through Gesture of Goodwill (GoG) payments for Authorized Push Payment (APP) fraud cases.

Customer and Business Benefits

For HSBC and Colleagues:
  • Regulatory Compliance – ECW meets regulatory requirements for fraud prevention, reinforcing HSBC’s leadership in financial security.
  • Reduced Fraud Losses – By preventing fraudulent transactions, ECW helps lower financial losses and operational costs.
  • Proactive Risk Management – The feature serves as evidence that HSBC has taken all possible measures to warn customers, strengthening trust and accountability.
For Customers:
  • Stronger Fraud Protection – Customers receive prompt alerts about potential scams before completing transactions.
  • Informed Decision-Making – The warning content educates customers on common fraud tactics, helping them make safer financial decisions.
